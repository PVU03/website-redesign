About the Parish

About Us

The name “Cabramatta” is Aboriginal itself, “Cabra” meaning a white grub and “Matta” meaning place.

Sacred Heart Cabramatta has always been a multicultural parish from the influx of migrants and refugees in 1946 all the way to the 1990s. The diverse backgrounds of past and present parishioners at Sacred Heart Cabramatta has always brought with it the Christian values of love, respect and tolerance. Together we have formed a true Parish Family.

Our Parish History

1949 - 1959

The first Catholic church at Cabramatta was an Australian Army hut placed in Park Road in 1919, with Fr Walsh from Liverpool coming here to officiate Holy Mass. When WWII came to an end on 1st February 1949, Cabramatta became its own parish under the patronage of the Sacred Heart. The buildings were used as classrooms on weekdays and as the Parish church on weekends. Our first Parish Priest was Fr Michael Joseph Kiely, who served Cabramatta from 1949 -1959 with Fr Daniel McAuliffe the first Assistant Priest, joining the parish in August 1956.

Sacred Heart School has always been an integral part of the Parish. The Sisters of Charity resided at St Mary’s convent in Liverpool and travelled daily by train to Cabramatta. When the new Presbytery was built on Park Road in 1953, Fr Kiely moved out of the cottage in Bartley Street where he had been living and this then became the first convent. The Sisters no longer had to travel from Liverpool every day.

The Polish Community: Post war migration of the early 1950’s brought about 700 Polish refugees to Cabramatta and its surrounding districts by 1956. At first, the spiritual needs of these predominantly Catholic people were met on an ad-hoc basis by visiting priests. A regular monthly Sunday Mass for the Polish community was inaugurated at Sacred Heart towards the end of 1952 when Fr Stanislaw Mika CSSR and Fr Arciszewski CM began to celebrate the 11am Polish Mass. Fr Sadok Mackowiak OP was the first permanent chaplain and remained so for seven years.

Fr Michael Kiely

1959 - 1969

In 1959, Fr Kiely left Cabramatta and Fr Darcy O’Keeffe was appointed as the second Parish Priest. He arrived in March 1959. Within twelve months, Fr O’Keeffe addressed the parishioners on the need for a new church and for new methods of financing it, which included the Planned Giving Scheme (Weekly Envelopes). Fr O’Keeffe was considered the driving force behind so much of the construction and financial practices of the Parish. He built onto the pioneering work of Father Kiely and the early pioneers and has left his mark on the tremendous growth and development of our Parish.

Approval from Archdiocese of Sydney to proceed with the construction of the new church using the Cruciform design. In early 1961 the Army Hut (Church) was moved to the rear of the church grounds (where the present Canteen & learning area is situated) and work commenced on the new church.

With the success of the Planned Giving Scheme, it was decided to continue with the Scheme and to build a new Convent at Park Road for the sisters to live in. This was opened in 1963.
Agreement was reached by the parishioners to continue with the fund raising through the Weekly Envelopes and to seal the previously dirt playground, to construct a Parish Hall, a School Canteen, Administration Block and add classrooms as required.

1969 - 1979

In September 1974, Fr O’Keeffe was appointed to Balgowlah Parish. He managed to receive approval from Cardinal Gilroy for an extension to the Presbytery before leaving. The Saint Vincent De Paul shop was added to the side of the Presbytery. Fr Patrick McAuliffe arrived at Cabramatta on 1st October 1974 and became our third Parish Priest.

25th Anniversary (1974) Our Parish celebrated its 25th Anniversary in 1974, which included a big mass that welcomed all cultures and nationalities, memorabilia inside the Church, and many festivities throughout the day.

The Vietnamese Community: Fr John Tran Quang Thien approached Fr McAuliffe for permission to have a Mass in Vietnamese held here at Cabramatta each week. Fr McAuliffe agreed and extended a warm welcome to the Vietnamese people. The first Vietnamese Mass here was on the 20th June 1982 at 4pm.

The Polish community continued to grow alongside with the Church. With its use of the new church, the Polish readily applied themselves to church cleaning duties after their first Friday Masses. Perhaps the most momentous and memorable visit by a Polish church dignitary to Cabramatta has been that of Cardinal Karol Wojtyla (Pope John Paul II) in February 1973.

The Spanish Community: The first Spanish Mass at Cabramatta was celebrated by Fr Gines Ortin 1974. It continued for a while however with the Spanish numbers dwindling, they continued to have Mass at Fairfield parish.

The Vietnamese Community: In 1977, Asians (Vietnamese, Chinese, Laotian, Thai, Malaysian and Cambodian) arrived in Cabramatta after the end of the Vietnam War. Visiting priests often came to hostels to say Mass, however many attended the English Masses in order to received Holy Communion. It was not until 1982 that the first Vietnamese Mass was said at Cabramatta.

The Italian Community: Father Angelo Buffalo celebrated the first Italian mass at Sacred Heart, Cabramatta, in 1977. This 7:30am Sunday Mass was the start to the building of the Italian community. The Italian community in Cabramatta was born, coming together with a common purpose to reinvigorate the faith for all the elderly, who perhaps had lost touch as a result of the language barriers and to introduce the young to the Italian traditions and to participate in the work of the Sacred Heart Parish. There was a great willingness of many lay people to participate, in particular the youth, who became the go-between that facilitated the breaking of the cultural barriers that existed.

1979 - 1989

Fr Paul Finucan was the first Cabramatta parishioner to be ordained as a priest on 22nd August 1981. He celebrated his first mass at Cabramatta with his family and the parish community.

In 1984, our Church underwent renovations following changes to Catholic worship and the celebration of mass post Vatican II. This included the removal of altar rails, relocation of the altar and having priests now face the congregation.

The Sisters of Charity continued to serve in our school and parish, with Sister Margaret Case joining the Parish Pastoral team in 1980 and Sister Jean Montgomery in 1985. Sister Enid Doherty was principal of the school from 1979 to 1985, followed by Sister Margaret Valentine in 1986. After 50 years, Sacred Heart School got its first lay Principal with the appointment of Mrs Shirley Jackson from 1988-1996, followed by Mrs Sybil Dickens.

The St Vincent De Paul’s Society had volunteers doing 20 – 30 home visits per week, helping with the influx of refugees from Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, South America and Poland. We were blessed to have the support and guidance of a spiritual advisor Father Mac and the assistance of the other parishioners. Over the years we have received generous support from Sacred Heart Primary School, Mary MacKillop College and Patrician Brothers’ College, and the anonymous benefactors of SVDP in Cabramatta.

The Vietnamese Community: Fr John Tran Quang Thien approached Fr McAuliffe for permission to have a Mass in Vietnamese held here at Cabramatta each week. Fr McAuliffe agreed and extended a warm welcome to the Vietnamese people. The first Vietnamese Mass here was on the 20th June 1982 at 4pm.

1989 - 1999

This was the decade of the Church’s Golden Jubilee. The decade started with Sacred Heart Parish getting actively involved in the archdiocesan program known as Parish 2000. Parish 2000 was a three-year strategy of parish renewal with aims to build a deep and continuous sense of community within the parish.

  • 1991 - Stage 1 - To gather the people
  • 1992 - Stage 2 - To listen to the Bible, ourselves, others, the community, the world.
  • 1993 - Stage 3 - To respond to this challenge as God’s people with energy and imagination


    • A Liturgy Committee was one of the recommendations. This required interested parishioners to work closely with the clergy to develop our liturgical celebrations.
    Some of the developments have come about – rostering commentators and readers at weekend Masses; intense planning for special occasions and masses; preparing notices; having organists and choirs to participate through music and hymns; having PowerPoint presentations and many more.

    Lenten Group: The Prayer Network Team which linked up people to pray for the renewal of the parish. The small groups began to gather in 1990; there were more groups meeting in 1991 growing to 23 by 1993.

    Acolytes and Special Minister: With the growth of the parish, the spiritual needs of parishioners also increased and additional ministers were required to assist the priests. In October 1978, Bishop Cremin installed the first acolytes into the Ministry of Acolyte at Cabramatta and in 1990 Extraordinary Ministers were also introduced.

    The Liturgy Team concentrated on making Sunday Masses, especially the 10am English mass, a family celebration. To this day, the Sunday 10am mass is known as the ‘Children’s mass’, with hymns being sung by the children’s choir.

    Fr Martin Nicol ran the RCIA process in Cabramatta, where he gathered a small team of catechists who were trained in the RCIA process. People who were interested in ﬁnding out more about the Catholic Faith were invited to join.
