About the Parish

About Us

The name “Cabramatta” is Aboriginal itself, “Cabra” meaning a white grub and “Matta” meaning place.

Sacred Heart Cabramatta has always been a multicultural parish from the influx of migrants and refugees in 1946 all the way to the 1990s. The diverse backgrounds of past and present parishioners at Sacred Heart Cabramatta has always brought with it the Christian values of love, respect and tolerance. Together we have formed a true Parish Family.

Our Parish History

1949 - 1959

The first Catholic church at Cabramatta was an Australian Army hut placed in Park Road in 1919, with Fr Walsh from Liverpool coming here to officiate Holy Mass. When WWII came to an end on 1st February 1949, Cabramatta became its own parish under the patronage of the Sacred Heart. The buildings were used as classrooms on weekdays and as the Parish church on weekends. Our first Parish Priest was Fr Michael Joseph Kiely, who served Cabramatta from 1949 -1959 with Fr Daniel McAuliffe the first Assistant Priest, joining the parish in August 1956.

Sacred Heart School has always been an integral part of the Parish. The Sisters of Charity resided at St Mary’s convent in Liverpool and travelled daily by train to Cabramatta. When the new Presbytery was built on Park Road in 1953, Fr Kiely moved out of the cottage in Bartley Street where he had been living and this then became the first convent. The Sisters no longer had to travel from Liverpool every day.

Post-war migration in the early 1950s brought around 700 Polish refugees to Cabramatta. The first permanent chaplain was Fr Sadok Mackowiak OP, who remained for 7 years.
