About the Parish

About Us

Welcome to Sacred Heart Parish, Cabramatta – a parish community immersed in history, enriched by diversity, and alive in faith. Located in the heart of South-West Sydney, Sacred Heart Cabramatta has been a spiritual home for people of all backgrounds, united by a common mission to share God’s love and mercy with our community.

Our Parish was built by the early visionaries who dreamed of a community where faith could flourish, and countless families have contributed their time, efforts, and resources to nurture and sustain the Parish community throughout the years. For over 75 years, our Parish has been a beacon of hope, a sanctuary in times of need, and a source of charity for all who have walked through its doors.

Our Parish Priest, Fr Remy Lam Bui, supported by Fr John Pham and the Sisters of Our Lady of the Southern Cross continues the exemplary history of service and generosity in Cabramatta, bringing God’s love and mercy to the community.

Our Parish History

Our Early Beginnings: Faith in Simplicity

The story of Sacred Heart Parish begins in September 1919, when the first Catholic church in Cabramatta was established using a humble Australian Army Hut purchased by His Grace Archbishop Kelly. Mass was celebrated by priests from Liverpool Parish, and during the week, the Sisters of Charity travelled by train to use the hut as a classroom for local children. On weekends, parishioners transformed the space for Sunday Mass.

Becoming a Parish: A Post-War Community

Following the tremendous growth in the region after World War II, Cabramatta was officially inaugurated as a parish on 1 February 1949. The first Parish Priest, Fr Michael Kiely, oversaw the early development of the parish. A cottage on Bartley Street was acquired in 1950, and a brick presbytery was built in 1952 on Park Road. This allowed the Sisters of Charity to move into community and begin a more permanent presence.

Migration and Multicultural Growth

Sacred Heart Parish has been uniquely shaped by successive waves of migration. Beginning in 1946, displaced people fleeing the devastation of war — primarily from Poland, Italy, Germany, and Yugoslavia — found a new spiritual home in Cabramatta. These European migrants formed the foundation of a faith-filled and hard-working community.

In the 1950s and 1960s, migrants from the United Kingdom further enriched parish life. In response to the community’s growth, local men formed a “Men’s Club” in 1957, contributing voluntary labour to build and maintain parish facilities.

Under Fr Darcy O’Keefe, who became the second Parish Priest in 1959, parish infrastructure expanded dramatically. A Canvass Committee was formed to raise funds through a planned giving scheme, while also welcoming new parishioners. Their efforts led to the construction of a new church, which was blessed on Christmas Eve 1961, with the first Mass celebrated by Cardinal Gilroy. The committee continued to raise funds for the acquisition of school grounds, building of a parish hall, school canteen, administrative block, and classrooms.

The 1980s and 1990s brought a profound transformation, as refugees and migrants from Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, and Thailand arrived in large numbers. Their presence reinvigorated the parish, leading to the introduction of Vietnamese-language liturgies, devotions, and new ministries. The Southeast Asian heritage continues to be central to Sacred Heart's cultural identity today.

Sacred Heart Catholic Primary School

Our connection to Catholic education remains a proud part of our parish heritage. Sacred Heart Catholic Primary School opened in January 1935 with an enrolment of 75 children. Since then, the school has grown alongside the parish, offering students an education grounded in faith, sacraments, and a sense of community. Close collaboration between parish and school continues today through shared liturgies, sacramental programs, and pastoral care.

Ministry and Outreach

Inspired by the motto of St Mary MacKillop, “Never see a need without doing something about it”, Sacred Heart Parish has a long and proud tradition of social outreach. For many decades the Parish operated the local St Vincent de Paul Society outreach supporting individuals and families in need through food assistance, home visits, and emergency relief. We have a generous team of catechists reaching out to children in our local state schools and bringing the Good News to their lives.

Youth and the Future

Our youth are not only the future — they are the present. Sacred Heart Parish boasts a vibrant and growing youth ministry, with active participation in Sunday liturgies, music ministry, retreats, social justice initiatives, and community events. Programs are designed to empower young people in their faith journey and offer meaningful ways to connect, serve, and nurture leadership for the future of our Parish.

Guided by Faithful Shepherds

Over the decades, Sacred Heart Parish has been blessed with visionary leadership. Fr O’Keefe, known for his drive and dedication, oversaw much of the parish’s physical growth. In 1974, Fr Patrick McAuliffe became the third Parish Priest who served as Parish Priest for over 40 years. He guided the parish through immense demographic changes and into the 21st century, always encouraging with his hallmark phrase: “Not a worry in the world.”

Today

Our late Holy Father, Pope Francis reminds us that the parish is "the normal environment where we learned to hear the Gospel, to know the Lord Jesus, to serve with gratuitousness, to pray in community, to share projects and initiatives, to feel part of God's holy people."
